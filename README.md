# CrisisPilot AI Emergency Co-pilot

CrisisPilot is an AI emergency co-pilot for families, small communities, and local teams.

It is designed for stressful moments when people do not need a complex dashboard — they need the next safe step.

## Product idea

During floods, earthquakes, power outages, injuries, or sudden evacuation events, most people struggle to decide what to do first. Information is scattered across weather alerts, local instructions, family group chats, maps, supply lists, and personal health needs.

CrisisPilot turns those signals into simple, one-tap action cards:

- find shelter
- handle injuries
- plan around power outages
- check in with family
- choose a safer route
- track supplies
- send status updates

## Why this is needed

Emergency tools are often built for institutions. CrisisPilot focuses on households and small groups.

It prioritizes:

- clarity over complexity
- large controls for panic moments
- offline-friendly instructions
- household check-ins
- plain-language risk translation
- next-step guidance instead of information overload

## Core workflow

1. **Understand**: ask only the minimum questions needed in a stressful moment.
2. **Prioritize**: rank actions by danger, time sensitivity, and available resources.
3. **Guide**: show simple instructions with large controls and offline fallback.
4. **Confirm**: track check-ins, next action, and whether the person is safe.

## AI architecture concept

CrisisPilot can combine:

- local risk signal parsing
- emergency instruction retrieval
- household context memory
- route and shelter ranking
- first-aid step generation from trusted sources
- multilingual plain-language summarization
- status-message generation for trusted contacts

## Suggested application description

I built CrisisPilot AI Emergency Co-pilot, a simple but futuristic emergency assistant for families and small local teams. The project solves a high-need problem: during floods, earthquakes, power outages, injuries, or sudden evacuations, people often receive too much fragmented information and do not know what to do first. CrisisPilot turns location risk, household status, supply levels, route warnings, and emergency guidance into one-tap action cards. The workflow is Understand, Prioritize, Guide, and Confirm. The assistant asks only the minimum questions needed, ranks actions by danger and time sensitivity, gives plain-language instructions, and tracks whether family members are safe. Example actions include finding shelter, guiding first aid, handling power outages, sending family check-ins, and choosing safer routes. The demo UI is designed for stressful moments: large buttons, clear priority alerts, a live safety mode phone interface, critical signal cards, and simple safety protocol steps. The long-term architecture includes risk signal parsing, trusted emergency instruction retrieval, household context memory, route/shelter ranking, multilingual summarization, and offline fallback guidance.
